Le Cinque — About

Le Cinque is a free game for learning beginner Italian vocabulary, five words at a time. Each round lays out five Italian words with their English translations hidden; you fill in the letters to reveal all five before you run out of mistakes. The curriculum runs to over 1,500 everyday words, ordered most-essential first, so beginners meet the core vocabulary soonest.

HOW A ROUND IS BUILT

Each round leads with the words that are due for review, then fills up with new ones you have not met yet. A round never mixes five words from a single category — five colours, five numbers — because closely related words learned together interfere with one another, so they are kept apart.

HOW REVIEW IS TIMED

Every time you get a word right it comes back later than before — after 1, then 3, 7, 21 and 60 days — because a word you keep recalling needs checking less often. Miss it and it drops back to a day, then climbs again. A word that stops being tested is a word being forgotten, so a word you got right still returns; it just returns further off. Already-learned words are never retired from the rotation.

HOW YOU GET CREDIT

You get credit for a word the moment you place one correct letter on it yourself. A correct letter is revealed across every word on the board, so a word can end up fully shown without your ever recalling it — and those do not count as learned. The point is to reward producing the word from memory, not seeing it appear.

THE LEARNING SCIENCE

The design follows the evidence on how vocabulary sticks. A few of the findings it leans on:

  • Retrieval, not re-reading. Trying to recall a word is what fixes it in memory; re-studying a word you already "know" does almost nothing. So the game tests you rather than showing you flashcards.
  • Produce, don't recognise. Recalling and spelling a word out beats picking it from a list of options, so you type the letters instead of choosing from a menu.
  • Spacing beats cramming, on an expanding schedule. The gap before a word returns grows as you keep getting it right, which is why the intervals stretch from a day out to two months.
  • Keep the answer honest. Credit is given only when you genuinely retrieve a word, so the count reflects what you can actually produce.
  • Cumulative review. Words are never dropped once learned — a word left untested is a word being forgotten.

The full reasoning, with citations to the underlying studies, is collected in the project's RESEARCH notes.
